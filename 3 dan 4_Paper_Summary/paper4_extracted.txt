Abstract
Eigenvector spatial пђБltering (ESF) is becoming a popular way to address
spatial dependence. Recently, a random effects speciпђБcation of ESF (RE-ESF) is
receiving considerable attention because of its usefulness for spatial dependence
analysis considering spatial confounding. The objective of this study was to analyze
theoretical properties of RE-ESF and extend it to overcome some of its disadvan-
tages. We пђБrst compare the properties of RE-ESF and ESF with geostatistical and
spatial econometric models. There, we suggest two major disadvantages of RE-ESF:
it is speciпђБc to its selected spatial connectivity structure, and while the current form
of RE-ESF eliminates the spatial dependence component confounding with ex-
planatory variables to stabilize the parameter estimation, the elimination can yield
biased estimates. RE-ESF is extended to cope with these two problems. A com-
putationally efпђБcient residual maximum likelihood estimation is developed for the
extended model. Effectiveness of the extended RE-ESF is examined by a com-
parative Monte Carlo simulation. The main пђБndings of this simulation are as fol-
lows: Our extension successfully reduces errors in parameter estimates; in many
cases, parameter estimates of our RE-ESF are more accurate than other ESF models;
the elimination of the spatial component confounding with explanatory variables
results in biased parameter estimates; efпђБciency of an accuracy maximization-based
conventional ESF is comparable to RE-ESF in many cases.

1 Introduction
Along with the recent development of geographic information systems (GIS),
statistical methods for spatial and spatiotemporal data have been discussed
extensively in geostatistics (e.g., Cressie 1993; Cressie and Wikle 2011) and
spatial econometrics (e.g., LeSage and Pace 2009). Geostatistical and spatial
econometric methods are useful because they consider properties of spatial data
such as spatial dependence and spatial heterogeneity. Spatial пђБltering (e.g., GrifпђБth
2010; GrifпђБth and Paelinck 2011; GrifпђБth and Chun 2013), which relates to both
geostatistics and spatial econometrics, is an approach that captures spatial
dependence using map pattern variables obtained from spatial connectivity
information (Getis and GrifпђБth 2002). The G statistic (Getis and Ord 1992)-based
пђБltering method of Getis (1990) and the Moran coefпђБcient (Moran 1950)-based
method called eigenvector spatial пђБltering (ESF; GrifпђБth 1996) are representative
methods. Applications of ESF are increasing because of its practicality, expand-
ability, and other appealing factors. For instance, Chun (2008), Fischer and GrifпђБth
(2008), GrifпђБth (2009, 2011), Chun and GrifпђБth (2011), Scherngell and Lata (2013),
and GrifпђБth and Fischer (2013) utilize ESF for spatial interaction analyses; Grimpe
and Patuelli (2009), Patuelli et al. (2011), and Cuaresma and Feldkircher (2013)
employ it for economic analyses; GrifпђБth and Peres-Neto (2006) and Jacob et al.
(2008) use it for ecological analyses; Helbich and Arsanjani (2014) and Chun
(2014) use it for criminal analyses; Wang et al. (2013) analyze land value and land
use applying it; and Moniruzzaman and Paez (2012) use it for urban design analysis.
Thus, ESF has become a popular method to address spatial dependence (Pace et al.
2013).
One of the advantages of ESF is simplicity (GrifпђБth 2003). The basic model of
ESF is identical to the standard linear and generalized linear regression models, and
therefore, it is easily implemented and extended, for example, by combining it with
standard non-spatial models (e.g., Poisson and logistic regression; see, e.g., GrifпђБth
2002, 2004a). Another advantage of ESF is its effectiveness in capturing spatial
dependence. Tiefelsdorf and GrifпђБth (2007) show that ESF effectively removes
spatial dependence from residuals, and GrifпђБth (2006) demonstrates its usefulness
for extracting negative spatial dependence hidden by predominant positive spatial
dependence. Thayn and Simanis (2013) show that ESF reduces spatial misspeciпђБ-
cation errors, increases the strength of a model пђБt, frequently increases the normality
of model residuals, and can increase the homoscedasticity of model residuals.
Franzese and Hays (2014) show the effectiveness of ESF in distinguishing a
common exposure effect, which is a source of spatial dependence, and a spatial
spillover effect, which is another source of spatial dependence. Some important

ESF) and demonstrate the usefulness for spatial dependence analysis while
mitigating the variance inпђВation due to the confounding factor between explanatory

The objective of this study was to analyze advantages and disadvantages of RE-
ESF, and extend it to overcome some of the disadvantages. This paperвАЩs sections are
organized as follows. Section 2 brieпђВy explains conventional ESF and RE-ESF.
Section 3 analyzes theoretical properties of ESF and RE-ESF models by comparing
them with models in geostatistics and spatial econometrics. Therein, we point to two
disadvantages of RE-ESF: it is sensitive to the chosen spatial connectivity structure,
and while the current form of RE-ESF eliminates spatial confounder, the
elimination can yield biased parameter estimates. Section 4 extends RE-ESF to
cope with these problems, and Sect. 5 compares ESF models, including our
extended RE-ESF, by a Monte Carlo simulation. Finally, Sect. 6 concludes our
discussion.

2.2 Standard ESF
ESF models spatial dependence using the eigenvectors of MCM in Eq. (1). The

the largest positive MC (depicting the maximum positive spatial dependence)

second eigenvector, e2, is the set of real values that has the largest positive MC that

The set of eigenvectors of MCM, Efull = {e1, вА¶, eN}, furnishes all possible distinct
map pattern descriptions of latent spatial dependence, with each level being indexed

7 Concluding remarks
This study пђБrst discusses the theoretical properties of conventional ESF models and
a RE-ESF version by Hughes and Haran (2013). There, we show that, although the

can be sensitive to the chosen spatial connectivity matrix, C. To mitigate the
dependency on C, we extend the RE-ESF to estimate the scale of a spatial

efпђБcient REML estimation for the model.
